Why Northern Rivers Properties Need Special Preparation

Living between the ocean and the hinterland, we cop weather from all directions. East coast lows, ex-tropical cyclones, and those sudden storms that appear from nowhere ‚Äì they all hit differently here.

The 2022 floods changed everything. Properties that had never flooded went under. "100-year" weather events are happening every few years. The old preparation rules don't apply anymore.

Critical Update Post-2022:

Flood modelling has changed dramatically. If you're relying on pre-2022 flood maps or "it's never flooded here before" thinking, you need to reassess. I've seen properties 5 meters above historical flood levels go under.

The 30-Point Storm Preparation Checklist

I've organized this by urgency ‚Äì tackle the critical items first, then work through the rest as time allows.

üî¥ CRITICAL - Do These First (48 Hours Before)

  • Clean ALL gutters and downpipes - Blocked gutters cause 60% of storm water damage
  • Secure or remove shade sails - They become destructive sails in high winds
  • Check roof for loose tiles/sheets - One loose tile becomes twenty in a storm
  • Clear storm water drains - Remove leaves, sticks, any blockages
  • Trim trees near house - Anything within falling distance needs attention
  • Test and photograph main water/gas shutoffs - You need to find these in the dark
  • Charge all devices and battery banks - Power often goes first
  • Fill bathtub and containers with water - Pumps fail when power goes

üü† HIGH PRIORITY - Complete Within 24 Hours

  • Move vehicles to high ground - Even 30cm of moving water will float a car
  • Secure all outdoor furniture - Stack and tie or bring inside
  • Check fence panels and gates - Loose palings become projectiles
  • Clear balcony/deck completely - Everything becomes a missile at 100km/h
  • Install temporary flood barriers - Sandbags at doors, plastic on air vents
  • Backup important documents - Photo everything, upload to cloud
  • Stock emergency supplies - See detailed list below
  • Create emergency communication plan - How will family members find each other?

üîµ IMPORTANT - If Time Allows

  • Photograph property condition - Essential for insurance claims
  • Lift valuables above potential flood height - Add 1 meter to "expected" levels
  • Disconnect non-essential appliances - Prevents surge damage
  • Seal important items in plastic - Double-bag electronics, photos
  • Prepare emergency lighting - Torches in every room, tested
  • Check insurance details - Know your coverage and claim process
  • Fill camping gas bottles - For cooking when power's out
  • Secure pool equipment - Loose pool covers are particularly dangerous
  • Board windows if severe - Plywood, not tape (tape doesn't work)
  • Clear yard of loose items - Kids toys, garden tools, pot plants
  • Check first aid supplies - Especially prescription medications
  • Prepare pets - Carriers, food, medication, comfort items
  • Test emergency radio - ABC North Coast 94.5 FM is your lifeline
  • Know your evacuation route - Have three options, higher ground preferred

Essential Storm Supplies (The Reality Check)

Forget the generic lists. Here's what you actually need based on real Northern Rivers storm experiences:

Power & Light

  • Battery banks: Minimum 20,000mAh per person
  • Head torches: Hands-free is essential (not phone torches)
  • Portable radio: Battery or crank powered
  • Glow sticks: Safe light for kids' rooms

Water & Food

  • Water: 10L per person minimum (not 3L like guidelines say)
  • Camp stove: With extra gas (BBQs flood)
  • Can opener: Manual (sounds obvious until you need it)
  • Instant coffee: Morale matters in disasters

The Forgotten Essentials

  • Cash: $500+ in small notes (EFTPOS dies with power)
  • Toilet paper: Lots (shops empty fast)
  • Medications: Two weeks supply
  • Mosquito repellent: They explode after floods
  • Rubber boots: Not thongs for flood water
  • Heavy duty garbage bags: Waterproofing everything

Local Knowledge:

In Byron/Ballina, the Woolworths and Coles empty within 2 hours of a severe weather warning. The smaller IGAs often have stock longer. Mullumbimby floods first, Byron Bay township rarely floods but loses power. Ocean Shores gets cut off quickly.

Property-Specific Preparations

For Beachside Properties

  • Windows cop horizontal rain ‚Äì seal frames with towels
  • Salt spray travels 500m inland in storms ‚Äì rinse everything after
  • Beach erosion can undermine structures ‚Äì check footings

For Hinterland Properties

  • Landslips are real ‚Äì know the signs (cracks, bulging)
  • Creek levels rise FAST ‚Äì never rely on "normal" behavior
  • Access roads flood first ‚Äì leave early or prepare to stay

For River/Creek Properties

  • Understand your catchment ‚Äì rain upstream matters more
  • King tides plus rain equals disaster ‚Äì check tide times
  • Floating debris destroys everything ‚Äì clear what you can

During the Storm

Safety Rules That Save Lives

  • Never walk through flood water - 15cm can knock you down
  • Stay away from windows - Even in "just wind" events
  • Don't use candles - Fire services can't reach you
  • Monitor ABC Radio - Social media spreads dangerous rumors
  • If roof damage occurs - Move to strongest room (usually bathroom)

Power Safety

  • Turn off main switch if flooding threatens
  • Never touch electrical equipment in wet areas
  • Assume all fallen lines are live
  • Don't use generators indoors (carbon monoxide kills)

After the Storm - The First 24 Hours

Immediate Actions

  1. Check for injuries - Adrenaline masks pain
  2. Document everything - Photos before touching anything
  3. Check structure safety - Cracks, movement, strange sounds
  4. Watch for wildlife - Snakes and spiders seek high ground too
  5. Begin water damage mitigation - First 48 hours are critical

What NOT to Do

  • Don't turn power on until checked by electrician
  • Don't drink tap water until cleared
  • Don't drive through water (road underneath might be gone)
  • Don't start repairs before insurance assessment

Health Warning:

Flood water is toxic sewage. Any cut exposed to flood water needs immediate medical attention. I've seen serious infections from tiny scratches. Wear boots, gloves, and assume everything touched by flood water is contaminated.

Insurance Preparation Tips

Having helped dozens with claims, here's what actually helps:

  • Video walk-through: Record everything, narrating items and conditions
  • Photograph serial numbers: All appliances and electronics
  • Keep purchase receipts: Digital copies in cloud storage
  • Know your policy: Flood vs storm water damage matters
  • Document pre-existing issues: So they're not blamed on storm

Building Resilience for Next Time

After each weather event, I see the same preventable damage. Consider these upgrades:

Quick Wins ($100-500)

  • Gutter guards (reduce blockage risk)
  • Window locks (prevent blow-open damage)
  • Surge protectors (on everything)
  • Emergency kit in waterproof container

Worth the Investment ($500-2000)

  • Proper storm shutters (not tape)
  • Backup battery system
  • Chainsaw (for exit clearing)
  • Submersible pump

Long-term Solutions ($2000+)

  • House raising (if flood-prone)
  • Generator connection point
  • Roof tie-down upgrade
  • Proper drainage system

The Reality Check

Perfect preparation isn't always possible. Life's busy, storms arrive suddenly, and sometimes you're caught off guard. Do what you can, prioritize safety over property, and remember ‚Äì everything can be repaired or replaced except people.

I've rebuilt hundreds of storm-damaged homes. The families who fare best aren't necessarily those with perfect preparation ‚Äì they're those who act early, help their neighbors, and maintain perspective when things go wrong.
