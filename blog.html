If you live near the beach, you'll know that maintenance needs are different from inland properties. Working around Byron Bay versus places like Lismore or Casino, the difference is obvious within the first year of any installation or repair.

Byron Bay's Salt Air Reality

Byron Bay's salt air is brutal on anything metal. I've seen stainless steel door handles corrode within 2 years near the beach front. The same handles inland would last 10+ years easily.

Recently I worked on a deck in Byron Bay where all the galvanized screws had corroded and stained the timber. The deck was only 3 years old, but looked terrible because nobody had told the owner to use marine-grade fixings near the coast.

What Works Better Near the Coast

From the decks I've worked on around Byron Bay, here's what lasts:

  • Marine-grade stainless steel for all screws and brackets
  • Aluminium over steel wherever possible
  • Quality primer and paint systems - not the budget stuff
  • Regular washing to remove salt buildup

It's not just salt air - the humidity around Byron Bay causes different problems than I see inland. Timber swells and contracts more, paint doesn't cure as well, and things like hinges get sticky faster.

One customer in Suffolk Park taught me about this - their kitchen cabinet doors were constantly sticking. Turned out the humidity was making the timber swell just enough to bind in the frames. Had to plane them back and seal the edges properly.

Seasonal Differences

During the 2024 wet season, I noticed way more emergency calls from coastal properties than inland ones. The combination of salt air, humidity, and constant moisture causes accelerated wear on everything.

Hinterland properties around Bangalow and Mullumbimby have different challenges - more bushfire preparation, different pest issues, but much less corrosion and salt damage.

What Coastal Properties Need More Often

  • Gutter cleaning: Salt and sea spray creates more corrosive buildup
  • Door and window maintenance: Salt air gets into mechanisms
  • Deck maintenance: UV plus salt air is a harsh combination
  • Metal component replacement: Hinges, handles, brackets corrode faster

Budget Reality for Coastal Homes

I always tell coastal property owners to budget about 30% more for maintenance than they would inland. It's not that I charge more - it's that things need attention more often.

A deck that might need oiling every 3 years in Lismore will need it every 18 months in Byron Bay. Door handles that would last 8 years inland might need replacing after 4 years near the beach.

The Upside

On the positive side, coastal properties rarely have the pest issues you see inland, there's usually good drainage from the sea breeze, and problems are often obvious earlier because salt damage shows up clearly.

The 10-Minute Buyer Walk-Through Test

Here's something I learned from a real estate agent in Byron Bay - buyers make up their minds in the first 10 minutes. They're looking for reasons to say no, not reasons to say yes.

So focus on things that will make them think "this place is well-maintained" rather than "I wonder what else is wrong here."

Byron Bay Market Expectations

Coastal properties have different standards. Buyers expect things to show some wear from salt air, but they want to see that it's been properly maintained.

Recently I worked on a property sale in Ocean Shores where we focused on:

  • Making sure all outdoor hardware was secure (salt air damage obvious)
  • Checking deck boards weren't loose or splintered
  • Ensuring all door and window locks worked smoothly
  • Fixing any obvious water damage or staining

Quick Fixes vs Big Projects

Most pre-sale work I do is small stuff that makes a big difference:

$50-150 fixes that help:

  • Tighten loose door handles and cabinet doors
  • Replace sticky or broken locks
  • Secure loose deck boards or railings
  • Fix basic electrical issues

$500+ jobs that usually aren't worth it:

  • Full deck restoration (unless structural issues)
  • Kitchen renovations (buyers often want their own style)
  • Cosmetic painting (unless covering damage)
  • New fixtures that work fine but look dated

Timeline Planning

Don't wait until the week before listing. I had a call from someone in Bangalow who wanted everything fixed in 3 days before their first open house. Some issues take time to source parts or need to dry/cure properly.

Give yourself at least 2 weeks to identify and fix issues properly.
