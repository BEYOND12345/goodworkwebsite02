Specialist knowledge of multi-dwelling door requirements

Fire Doors in Units and Apartments

Fire doors are a critical safety feature in every unit complex across Tweed Heads, and they're one of the most commonly neglected maintenance items. These doors are engineered to contain fire and smoke, and they only work if they're maintained properly. A fire door that doesn't close fully, has damaged seals, or has been propped open with a doorstop is not doing its job.

I inspect, repair and maintain fire doors in unit complexes across Tweed Heads, Tweed Heads South, Banora Point and Kingscliff. Strata managers and body corporates contact me regularly for fire door maintenance because I understand the compliance requirements and provide proper documentation for their records.

  • Self-closing mechanisms that work reliably
  • Intumescent seals intact and undamaged
  • Smoke seals in good working condition
  • No gaps greater than 3mm around the frame
  • Latches engaging properly on closure
  • Door not modified from original specification

NSW Building Requirements for Doors

Making sure your doors meet the necessary standards

Doors in NSW need to meet certain building standards, particularly in multi-dwelling buildings like the unit complexes that are common throughout Tweed Heads. As a handyman who works on doors regularly, I make sure all my repairs and installations comply with the relevant requirements. Here's what you should know:

Fire Door Requirements

Fire doors in multi-dwelling buildings must maintain their fire rating at all times. This means the door, frame, hardware, seals and closing mechanism all need to work together as a system. Any modification to a fire door - even something that seems minor like replacing a lock or adding a viewer - needs to be done carefully to maintain the fire rating.

  • Must self-close and latch from any open position
  • Intumescent and smoke seals must be intact
  • No unapproved modifications or penetrations
  • Maximum 3mm gaps around the frame
  • Regular maintenance and inspection required

Accessibility Requirements

Commercial and public buildings in Tweed Heads need to meet accessibility standards for doors. Even if you're not doing a major renovation, door repairs should maintain or improve accessibility where required.

  • Minimum clear opening widths maintained
  • Door handles operable with one hand
  • Door closers adjusted to reasonable force
  • Threshold transitions kept minimal
  • Contrast marking where required

Security Requirements

Rental properties in NSW have minimum security requirements for entry doors. Landlords in Tweed Heads need to ensure that entry doors have functioning deadlocks or equivalent security, and that all locks work properly with keys provided to tenants.

  • Functioning deadlock on all entry doors
  • All keys provided to tenants at lease start
  • Locks rekeyed between tenancies on request
  • Screen and security doors maintained
  • Sliding door locks operational

Strata By-Law Compliance

Many unit complexes in Tweed Heads have by-laws that govern what modifications owners can make to doors, what types of screen doors are permitted, and how common area doors must be maintained. Before doing any door work in a strata property, I check the relevant by-laws to make sure the work complies.

  • Screen door style and colour requirements
  • Security door approval processes
  • Common property maintenance obligations
  • Noise reduction requirements for entry doors
  • Approval needed for visible external changes

If you're unsure about the requirements for your specific situation in Tweed Heads, give me a call. I can advise on what standards apply and make sure any work I do meets them. It's always easier and cheaper to get it right the first time than to redo work that doesn't comply.
