Window Problems Specific to Ballina & Lismore

Inland and river areas create different challenges than the coast. Here's what I deal with most in your area.

Flooding Damage to Windows

After the major floods that hit Lismore and surrounding areas, window damage was one of the most common repair jobs I dealt with. Floodwater does serious damage to windows even if it only reaches sill height.

The water gets into timber frames and saturates them. As the timber dries out it warps, cracks and often develops mould. Hardware gets clogged with silt and corrodes. Even aluminium window tracks can be jammed with flood debris that's incredibly difficult to remove without proper tools.

Flood damage repairs I handle:

  • Swollen and warped timber frame repair or replacement
  • Silt and debris removal from tracks and mechanisms
  • Corroded hardware replacement
  • Mould treatment and prevention on timber frames
  • Complete window rebuild when frames are beyond repair
  • Insurance documentation and photo evidence

Humidity Swelling Timber Frames

Even without flooding, the humidity in the Ballina and Lismore areas causes ongoing problems with timber window frames. The low-lying terrain and proximity to rivers means moisture levels are consistently high, especially through the warmer months.

Timber frames absorb this moisture and swell. Suddenly your window that opened fine in winter is jammed shut in summer. Over time the constant swelling and contracting cycle causes joints to loosen, paint to crack, and eventually rot to take hold.

Signs of humidity damage:

  • Windows that are harder to open in humid weather
  • Paint cracking or peeling on timber frames
  • Visible gaps between frame joints when dry
  • Dark staining on the inside of window frames
  • Musty smell near windows, especially in the morning

Termite Damage to Window Frames

Termites are a real problem in the Ballina, Lismore and Alstonville areas. The warm, humid conditions are ideal for termite colonies, and timber window frames are a favourite target. I've seen plenty of window frames that look fine from the outside but are completely hollowed out by termites inside.

The tricky thing with termite damage is that by the time you notice it, the damage is usually quite extensive. A window latch might stop holding because the timber it's screwed into has been eaten away. Or a frame might crack when you try to open the window because there's nothing structural left inside.

  • Assessment of termite damage extent
  • Removal of damaged timber sections
  • Replacement with termite-resistant treated timber
  • Coordination with pest control for active infestations
  • Preventative treatment of adjacent timber
  • Full frame replacement when damage is too extensive to repair

Older Aluminium Windows in 70s-80s Homes

If you live in Ballina, East Ballina, West Ballina or the older parts of Lismore, there's a good chance your home has original aluminium windows from when it was built in the 1970s or 1980s. These windows were built to last and most of them are still structurally sound, but after 40-50 years the moving parts need attention.

The rollers that the sliding sash sits on wear down over time. The tracks oxidise and develop a rough surface that makes sliding difficult. Latches corrode and either seize shut or won't catch at all. Weatherstrips that once kept out drafts have long since perished.

  • Roller replacement for smooth sliding action
  • Track cleaning and deoxidising
  • Latch and lock replacement with compatible hardware
  • Weatherstrip replacement for draft-proofing
  • Adjustment of sash height and alignment
  • Sourcing discontinued parts or finding compatible alternatives
