What Screen Problems Do I Fix?

From small patches to complete re-meshing, I handle all types of fly screen repairs

Torn Fly Screen Mesh

This is the big one. Mesh gets torn by pets, kids, storms, or sometimes it just gives up after years of our coastal weather.

I can patch small tears, or completely re-mesh the whole screen if it's had it. I've got different types of mesh too:

  • Standard fibreglass mesh
  • Tougher aluminum mesh
  • Heavy-duty pet-resistant mesh
  • Security mesh for extra protection
  • Solar mesh for heat reduction

Screen Doors That Won't Close Properly

Screen doors cop a hiding in our climate. Kids slamming them, adults trying to get through with shopping, and our weather warping the frames.

Common issues I fix:

  • Doors that sag and won't line up with the frame
  • Hinges that squeak or have seized up
  • Handles and latches that don't work smoothly
  • Frames that have warped in the heat
  • Bottom rails that have rotted out from moisture

Sliding Screen Doors

When sliding screens stop sliding, it's usually because:

  • The track is full of sand, dirt and dead insects
  • The rollers have worn out or seized
  • The door keeps jumping off the track
  • The mesh has come loose from the frame
  • The door won't stay closed and slides open by itself

I can get these sliding smoothly again, plus show you how to keep them working properly.

Security screens are an investment, so when they're damaged you want them fixed properly:

Why Do Screens Get Damaged So Much Around Here?

Understanding the problem helps prevent it happening again

Our Coastal Weather

The salt air looks beautiful but it's tough on screens. It corrodes metal and makes mesh brittle. Add in our storms and it's a recipe for screen damage.

Constant Use

We use our screen doors constantly in this climate. All that opening and closing adds up to serious wear and tear on hinges, handles, and mesh.

The Wildlife

The very bugs we're trying to keep out can damage screens. Plus fruit bats, birds, and if you're unlucky, possums deciding your screen door is in their way.

UV Damage

Our intense sun breaks down everything over time. Mesh becomes brittle, frames fade, rubber seals perish. It's unavoidable but manageable with quality materials.
