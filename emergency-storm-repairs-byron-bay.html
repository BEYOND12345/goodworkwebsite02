Emergency Storm Repairs Byron Bay - What to Do First

Cyclone season hits Byron Bay hard. February-March especially - we get these massive storm cells rolling in from the ocean. 100km/h winds, driving rain sideways, hail. Your roof lifts, windows smash, fence blows over, water pours into your house.

Here's what to do when a storm damages your Byron Bay property.

⚠️ URGENT - Do These FIRST

If water's actively coming in or property isn't secure:

  • Turn off power at the main switchboard if water's near electrics
  • Stop water coming in - tarp the roof, block broken windows with boards/plastic
  • Secure your property - broken doors/windows are security risks

Fix These Issues IMMEDIATELY (Same Day)

Some damage can wait. Some damage gets exponentially worse every hour. Here's what needs fixing right now:

1. Active Roof Leaks

Water coming through your ceiling will:

  • Destroy ceiling plaster (soggy, collapses, Contact for quote-3,000 to replace)
  • Rot ceiling joists (structural, Contact for quote-8,000)
  • Damage electrics (dangerous, expensive)

Proper fix: Replace damaged roof sheeting, flashing, or tiles. Byron Bay roofs cop a beating - iron rusts through from salt air, tiles crack from hail and heat cycles. Cost: Contact for quote-2,500 for typical storm damage repairs.

Can't get on your roof safely? Don't. Call someone. Broken leg costs more than a callout.

2. Broken Windows or Doors

Broken glass = two problems:

  • Security - anyone can walk in
  • Weather - rain/wind blowing through ruins everything

Temporary fix: Board it up. 12mm ply from hardware store, screw it over the opening from inside. Or heavy plastic sheeting if you need light.

Proper fix: Replace the glass or window. Byron glaziers are usually booked solid for weeks after a big storm - everyone's got broken windows. If you can source the glass yourself and it's a simple frame, I can fit it same-day.

3. Compromised Security

Doors blown in, locks broken, windows smashed - your property's not secure.

Priority fix because Byron has property crime. Opportunists drive around after storms looking for unsecured houses.

Temporary: Board it, padlock what you can, get someone to stay there if it's really bad.

Byron Bay Storm Damage Patterns

After every cyclone season, I see the same areas get hit:

Belongil/beachfront: Cop the worst wind. Roofs lift (especially older iron roofs with rusty screws), windows smash from flying debris, fences blow down. East-facing properties get smashed.

Suffolk Park/Sunrise: High exposure. Lots of deck damage from wind, outdoor furniture destroyed, pergola damage common. Trees come down on houses fairly regularly.

Byron town: More sheltered but still get damage. Mainly older buildings - roof tiles, window damage, fences. Heritage buildings around Jonson Street cop it.

Hinterland (Bangalow Road, Ewingsdale): Big trees = falling branches. Deck damage from debris. Road access blocked by fallen trees.

Insurance Claims - Document Everything

Do this before fixing anything major:

  • Photos of all damage - multiple angles, close-ups, wide shots showing context
  • Video walkthrough - especially water damage (shows extent better than photos)
  • Keep damaged materials - insurance may want to inspect broken tiles, wood, etc.
  • Get quotes in writing - most Byron insurers want written quotes before approving repairs
  • Keep all receipts - even for tarps and temporary fixes

Byron Bay post-storm, every tradie and builder is slammed. Insurance assessors are booked 2-3 weeks out. Document everything yourself immediately - don't wait for the assessor.

Preventing Storm Damage (Before Next Season)

February-March is cyclone season. Do this before summer:

Roof Maintenance:

  • Check all roof screws - replace rusty ones with marine stainless (iron roofs lift when screws corrode)
  • Replace cracked or broken tiles
  • Re-seal flashing around chimneys, vents
  • Clean gutters (blocked gutters overflow, water gets under roof)

Windows & Doors:

  • Check all latches work properly
  • Replace perished window seals
  • Security screens (protect glass from debris)
  • Storm shutters for beachfront properties (expensive but worth it)

Fence & Structures:

  • Check fence posts are solid - replace wobbly ones
  • Reinforce gates
  • Trim trees back from house (big branches = roof damage)
  • Secure outdoor furniture (it becomes flying missiles in 100km/h wind)

Deck Safety:

  • Check all deck boards are screwed down properly
  • Check structure underneath isn't rotten
  • Secure or store deck furniture before storms hit

When to Call Emergency Services vs A Handyman

Call SES (132 500):

  • Tree fallen on house
  • Major structural damage
  • Roof completely gone
  • Flooding inside property
  • Power lines down

Call a handyman/tradie:

  • Roof leaking but structure sound
  • Window/door damage
  • Fence down
  • Deck damage
  • Gutter damage
  • Minor to moderate repairs

SES is free but they're volunteers and get slammed during storms. They do emergency stuff only - make it safe, not fix it properly. Then you need a tradie for permanent repairs anyway.
