Warning Signs You Can't Ignore

Spongy Deck Boards

Deck boards feel soft when you walk on them - classic sign of internal rot eating away the timber structure.

Wobbly Posts

Posts move when you push against them - structural integrity is compromised and safety is at risk.

Easy Penetration

You can stick a screwdriver into the timber easily - the rot has progressed beyond surface level.

Peeling Paint

Paint peeling with grey/black timber underneath - moisture has penetrated and rot is active.

Bouncy Deck

Your deck "bounces" more than it used to - support structure is failing from rot damage.

The Science Behind Coastal Timber Repair

Understanding why regular timber fails in Byron Bay - and what actually works long-term

Why H4 Treatment Matters

H4 Hazard Level 4 is specifically designed for high moisture environments. Here's what makes it different:

  • Copper Azole (CuAz) - Protects against fungal decay and termites
  • Deep Pressure Treatment - Preservative penetrates to the core, not just surface coating
  • Moisture Resistance - Designed for direct ground contact and coastal conditions
  • Salt Air Tolerance - Won't break down from salt crystallization like untreated timber
  • Property Management

What Doesn't Work in Byron Bay:

  • H3 treated pine (indoor/covered outdoor only)
  • Untreated hardwood (even naturally durable species fail here)
  • DIY stains and sealers (temporary protection only)
  • Galvanized screws (salt air causes corrosion)
  • Property Management

Dan's Coastal Timber Checklist

• H4 CuAz treated hardwood
• 316 marine grade stainless steel fasteners
• Proper drainage design
• End-grain sealing
• Adequate ventilation spacing

Byron Bay Specific Challenges

Salt Air Corrosion

Coastal salt accelerates metal corrosion and breaks down timber cell structure. Regular galvanized fittings fail within 2-3 years.

Extreme Humidity

Byron's humidity creates perfect conditions for fungal growth. Moisture levels above 20% trigger rapid timber decay.

Storm Damage

Seasonal storms drive water into timber joints. Poor drainage design leads to water pooling and accelerated rot.

Small repairs start around $300. Most deck board replacements are $800-1,500. Major structural work can be $2,000-5,000. I'll give you exact pricing after I assess your specific situation.
Q: What timber do you use for coastal repairs?
I use H4 treated hardwood for structural elements and marine-grade fasteners. No shortcuts when it comes to coastal conditions.

With proper materials and installation, you should get 15-20 years from structural repairs, 10-15 years from decking boards.
